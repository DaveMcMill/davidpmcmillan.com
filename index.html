Most strategies don't fail in the boardroom.
They fail in the hand-off.

  • Leadership agrees on direction — then it stalls across business units, agencies, or partners who were never truly aligned.
  • Initiatives multiply faster than anyone can govern them, and no one can say which ones actually matter.
  • KPI dashboards exist, but they measure activity, not the decisions that move the strategy forward.
  • Transformation programs launch with energy and lose it within two quarters, once the complexity of multiple stakeholder groups sets in.

If any of this sounds familiar, the gap isn't your strategy. It's the execution architecture underneath it.
